# Service Brake Valve

**Equipment:** JLG G5-18A

1. Park the machine on a firm, level surface, fully retract the boom, lower the boom, place the transmission in (N) NEUTRAL, engage the park brake and shut the engine OFF.
2. Place a Do Not Operate Tag on both the ignition key switch and the steering wheel.
3. Open the engine cover. Allow the system fluids to cool.
4. Check the routing of all hoses, and tubing for sharp bends or interference with any rotating members, and install tie wraps and/or protective conduit as required. Tighten all tube and hose clamps.
5. Remove the necessary dash panels.

8. Inspect the service brake valve and connections for leaks, and check the brake oil level. Shut the engine OFF.
9. Replace the necessary dash panels. 10. Wipe up any hydraulic oil spillage in, on, near and around the machine, work area and tools. 11. Close and secure the engine cover. 12. Remove the Do Not Operate Tags from both the ignition key and the steering wheel.  c.   Service Brake Bleeding Procedure  Carefully bleed the brake lines as soon as the brake valve is installed on the machine. Air in the system will not allow the brakes to apply properly. There are two brake bleeder locations ( 10 ) on the front axle. The outside bleeder is used for the park brake circuit. The inside bleeder is used for the service brake circuit. Work with an assistant to perform this procedure.

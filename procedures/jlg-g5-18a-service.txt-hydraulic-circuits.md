# HYDRAULIC CIRCUITS

**Equipment:** JLG G5-18A

1. Check the hydraulic oil level in the reservoir. If oil is cold, oil level should be visible in the lower gauge window with all cylinders retracted.
2. Check hoses, tubes, fittings and other hydraulic components for leaks, bends, kinks, interference, etc.
3. Check for air in the hydraulic system. Erratic machine performance and/or spongy cylinder and/or brake operation are signs of air in the hydraulic system. Loose fittings, faulty o-rings or seals, trapped oil, leaks, system opened for service, etc., can cause air in the system. Determine what is causing air to enter the system and correct it.

134  SFC 353-MegaJector Delivery Pressure Higher Than Expected     MegaJector     Functional Fault-Actual pressure is higher than commanded pressure by 4” H2O or more     MIL-On during active fault     Adaptive-Disabled during active fault     Closed Loop-Enabled   Power Derate (level1) The MegaJector is an electronic pressure regulator that is used to control fuel pressure on gaseous fueled engines. 135  SFC 353-MegaJector Delivery Pressure Higher Than Expected  Note:   If any other SFCs are present diagnose those first.  Diagnostic Aids  Fuel Supply – Check fuel supply pressure at the Megajector inlet fitting. Fuel supply pressure on LPG applications should be between 3”-5” H2O and between 8”-15” H2O on Natural Gas applications. Gaseous Fuel Lock off – Check the lock off to make sure that is sealing when closed. If it is not completely sealing it could cause pressure creep in the fuel system. Reference Line – Make sure that the reference line is in place between the MegaJector and the carburetor balance port. Make sure that the hose is not kinked or restricted in any way and has no holes in it. Gaseous Fuel Regulator – Observe the regulator with the engine running to see if it icing up. If it is, see the notes below. Engine Cooling System – Make sure the engine cooling system is operating properly and there are no air locks in the system. Make sure the engine is operating at the proper temperature. Check the coolant hoses at the regulator and make sure that they are both warm to verify proper coolant circulation. If fuel system is out of specification, service according to Ford Power Products guidelines and retest. If fuel system is operating properly, the MegaJector has an internal failure and must be replaced. Contact Ford Power Products for further information.
